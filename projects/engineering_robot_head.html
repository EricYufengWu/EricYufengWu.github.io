UR5 Arm System Robot Head

Fall 2019

As part of my ongoing research project with Professor Jivko Sinapov at Tufts University, I am building a robot head with 3 degrees of freedom for a UR5 Robot arm system in the lab. Currently, the mechanism consists of a stepper motor and two large servos. The plan is to mount a kinect camera on top of the head and control it in a 3D space using arduino.

In the end, although I wasn't able to fully finish the physical head, I gained valuable experience in applying prototyping skills and learning the basics of Robot Operating System (ROS).

Skills used:

Solidworks

Rapid Prototyping

ROS (Basic), converting Solidworks assembly to URDF file

Arduino, breadboarding, etc.


Version 1

As I went through the basic ROS tutorial, I started designing the first version of the actual head. It is a 3 degrees-of-freedom mechanism consists of one stepper motor and 2 servos, capable of rotating a kinect camera mounted on top of the head.

Design and Prototyping


Version 2

The first design requires too much torque from the horizontal servo when lifting the kinect up. Therefore, in this version I tried to bring the center of mass of the top part closer to the axis of rotation to improve the performance of the servos.

Design Sketch for Version 2


Solidworks Design


By this point, I have finished working on the ROS basic tutorial and URDF tutorial; therefore, I changed my focus from hardware to the software aspect. I started by converting the solidworks model into Collada files. Then, I re-assembled all the parts back to a single urdf file and visualized the joints in RVIZ.
