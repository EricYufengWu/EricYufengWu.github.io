Delta Ceramics 3D Printer

Note: This is an ongoing project, so I will be frequently updating the page.

Starting from July 2019, I am building a Delta-style ceramics 3D Printer with Floor Van De Velde, a Professor from the School of Museum of Fine Arts at Tufts (link to bio). For the first version of our printer, we adapted the design from Jonathan Keep, while making modifications and improvements on several apects, including the three sliding brackets at the sides, the rods fixturing methods, the upper plates, etc.

After we did the structure fabrication together, I connected the electronics and configured the Marlin Firmware. We printed out several vases successfully on the first week of September, and have decided to continue working on improving it's performance in all aspects.

This project gets me exposed to all aspects about a custom-build FDM 3D Printer, including stepper motor controls, power management, printer calibration, G-code, and slicing profiles.

Fluidic Control Board for Soft Robotics

I built a control board with a solenoid valve manifold and power MOSFETS. The board is used in my lab to study and test out inflatable soft robotics actuators.
